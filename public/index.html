1. Compare the Two Consensus Benchmarks

Look for deltas between the Nasdaq Estimate (current Zacks consensus from Nasdaq.com) and the Polymarket Target (Seeking Alpha consensus frozen at contract creation). A wide delta indicates that analyst expectations have shifted or differ between platforms, which can signal mispriced opportunities.

2. Cross-Reference Options Market

Polymarket reflects the probability of a beat, not the magnitude of price movement. Cross-reference the contract probability with the options Implied Move (Implied Volatility). Skewed option prices or heavy put buying signals market concern over forward guidance, regardless of a high Polymarket beat probability.

3. The "Forward Guidance" Blindspot

Polymarket contracts resolve strictly on headline EPS or revenue numbers. However, stock prices react heavily to Q2/FY outlooks. A company might easily beat Q1 estimates but sell off immediately if they issue soft forward guidance. Consider fading initial algorithmic headline spikes if outlook indicators are weak.

4. Signal Strength (Liquidity Check)

Prediction market signals are directly tied to contract volume and liquidity. Shallow liquidity pools (e.g. low volume contracts) represent retail speculation rather than institutional capital. A low-volume contract odds signal is weak and should be stress-tested against macro sector trends.
